EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

This research addresses how blockchain technology can enhance our healthcare
platform's data integrity, security, auditability, and controlled data sharing.

KEY FINDING: Blockchain provides significant value for governance and trust,
but should be used in a hybrid architecture with traditional systems handling
performance-critical operations.

RECOMMENDATION: Implement Hyperledger Fabric-based private blockchain for
consent management and audit trails, with 24-month phased rollout.

INVESTMENT: $350K initial, $115K/year ongoing | ROI: Positive by Year 2-3

KEY RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

WHERE TO USE BLOCKCHAIN (High Value):
✅ Consent management - Patient-controlled, auditable
✅ Audit trails - Immutable, tamper-proof compliance logs
✅ Inter-provider sharing - Trustless data exchange
✅ Data integrity - Cryptographic verification
✅ AI accountability - Traceable model decisions

WHERE NOT TO USE BLOCKCHAIN (Low Value):
❌ Primary data storage - Use off-chain storage (S3, database)
❌ High-frequency operations - Use traditional systems (>100 TPS)
❌ Real-time monitoring - Use streaming/time-series databases
❌ Simple internal apps - Traditional access control sufficient

ARCHITECTURE: Hybrid (blockchain for trust, traditional for performance)
PLATFORM: Hyperledger Fabric (private/consortium)
APPROACH: Phased (start with consent management pilot)

RISK ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

HIGH RISKS (Mitigate):
  ⚠️ Regulatory non-compliance → Early regulator engagement, HIPAA-compliant
  ⚠️ Data breach → Defense in depth, encryption, audits
  ⚠️ Smart contract bugs → Formal verification, code audits
  ⚠️ Performance issues → Hybrid architecture, load testing
  
MEDIUM RISKS (Monitor):
  ⚠️ Governance disputes → Clear framework, dispute resolution
  ⚠️ Integration complexity → Middleware, phased approach
  ⚠️ Expertise shortage → Training, specialists, managed services
  
LOW RISKS (Accept):
  ✓ Technology obsolescence
  ✓ Vendor lock-in
  ✓ User adoption
